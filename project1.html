Dual ±5V Power Supply

Status: Completed✅

Project Overview

A simple regulated dual power supply built using 7805 and 7905 voltage regulators. This power supply is designed to provide +5V and −5V DC rails for Op-Amp circuits and other analog electronics.

This was my first hands-on power electronics project where I practiced rectification, filtering, voltage regulation and veroboard soldering. I successfully tested the output voltages using a multimeter.

Technical Specifications

  • Transformer Input: 12V-0-12V AC
  • Outputs: +5V DC and −5V DC
  • Regulators: 7805 & 7905
  • Rectification: Bridge Diode
  • Board: Veroboard (Hand Soldered)

Components Used

  • 7805 Voltage Regulator
  • 7905 Voltage Regulator
  • 4 × 1N4007 Diodes
  • 1000µF Filter Capacitor
  • 470µF Output Capacitors
  • 100nF Bypass Capacitors
  • 12-0-12V Transformer
  • LED Indicators
  • Veroboard, Soldering Materials

Learning Outcomes

  • Worked with AC to DC conversion
  • Understanding voltage regulators and filtering
  • Multimeter testing for proper voltage output
  • Soldering and PCB layout basics

Challenges & Fixes

Issue: Negative voltage rail was unstable initially

Fix: Corrected diode orientation + placed bypass capacitors closer

Issue: Layout mistakes while soldering

Fix: Rearranged wiring for cleaner connection

Applications

Useful for powering:

  • Operational Amplifier Circuits
  • Analog Test Benches
  • Signal Processing Experiments
